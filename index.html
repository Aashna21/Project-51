Interesting facts about Camels

  • A camel is an even-toed ungulate in the genus Camelus that bears distinctive fatty deposits known as "humps" on its back.
  • Camels have three sets of eyelids and two rows of eyelashes to keep sand out of their eyes.
  • A thirsty camel can drink 30 gallons in 13 minutes.

Interesting facts about Chameleons

  • Chameleons are animals that are part of the iguanas sub-order.
  • Changing skin color is an important part of communication among chameleons.
  • Most chameleons have a prehensile tail that they use to wrap around tree branches.


Interesting facts about Pigeons

  • Pigeons are extremely intelligent and complex animals.
  • Pigeons are renowned for their outstanding navigational abilities.
  • Pigeons have excellent hearing abilities.


Interesting facts about Teddy Bears

  • Teddy bears were named after Teddy Roosevelts reluctance to shoot a bear cub while on a hunting trip in 1902.
  • The first toy stuffed bear was created by German toymaker Margarete Steiff.
  • The world's smallest stitched teddy bear is 0.29 inches tall, created by South African artist Cheryl Moss.
